# EspressoPi Dialing In Guide

**How to read your shots and adjust to dial in the perfect espresso.**

---

## Understanding Your Shot Metrics

### The 5 Key Numbers

Every shot gives you 5 critical pieces of information:

1. **Shot Time** (seconds) — How long from first drop to stop
2. **Pressure Curve** (bar) — How much pressure your machine is applying
3. **Flow Rate** (ml/sec) — How fast water is flowing through
4. **Temperature** (°C) — Brew temperature of the water
5. **Output Weight** (grams) — How much espresso came out

### The Ideal Shot

**Golden ratios:**
- **Time:** 25-30 seconds (rule of thumb)
- **Pressure:** Ramps to 9 bar, holds or declines slightly
- **Flow:** 2-4 ml/sec (depends on profile)
- **Temperature:** Within 1-2°C of your goal
- **Output:** Usually 1:2 ratio (18g in → 36g out)

## Reading Your Pressure Chart

The pressure curve tells you EVERYTHING about what's happening to your puck.

### What the Curve Shows

```
TIME (seconds) →
│
9 bar ├─────────────────────────── (plateau)
      │    ╱───────────┐
6 bar │   ╱             └──────    (decline)
      │  ╱
3 bar │ ╱ (ramp)
      │╱
0 bar ├────────────────────────
      0    5    10    15    20    25    30
```

**Phase 1: Preinfusion (0-6 sec)**
- Pressure rises slowly to 3-4 bar
- Water is soaking into the coffee puck
- Puck is NOT yet at equilibrium
- Goal: Saturate the puck evenly

**Phase 2: Rise (6-15 sec)**
- Pressure climbs from 4 to 9 bar
- Water is being forced through saturated puck
- Extraction begins
- Goal: Smooth, consistent climb (no jumps)

**Phase 3: Plateau (15-25 sec)**
- Pressure holds steady at target (usually 9 bar)
- Sweet spot of flavor extraction
- Aim for longest time here
- Goal: Balance sweetness vs acidity

**Phase 4: Decline (25-30 sec)**
- Pressure drops as puck erodes/water speeds up
- Removes bitter notes from over-extraction
- Too fast = sour (acidic)
- Too slow = bitter (overextracted)
- Goal: Controlled, intentional decline

## Diagnosing Common Problems

### Problem 1: GUSHER (water sprays everywhere)

**Looks like:**
- Pressure spikes suddenly or stays low
- Shot time WAY too short (< 15 seconds)
- Flow rate jumps to 5+ ml/sec
- Output is mostly water, not espresso

**What's happening:**
- Grind is TOO COARSE
- Water finds a path of least resistance
- Puck isn't holding back water

**How to fix:**
1. **Grind finer** — Go smaller on your grinder
2. **Tamp harder** — More pressure during tamping
3. **Distribute better** — Use WDT (Weiss Distribution Technique) or distribution tool to break up clumps
4. **Check beans** — Very light roasts can gush; might need different profile

**Test shot:** After adjusting, expect time to jump from 15 sec to 25+ sec

---

### Problem 2: CHANNELING (water flows in one spot)

**Looks like:**
- Gushing in first 10 seconds then dry
- Uneven output (spray on one side, nothing on other)
- Pressure spikes then drops suddenly
- Shot time inconsistent

**What's happening:**
- Water found a hole in the puck
- Rest of puck isn't extracting
- Usually caused by uneven distribution or tamp

**How to fix:**
1. **Distribute more carefully** — Use WDT tool before tamping
2. **Tamp level and straight** — Any tilt creates weak spots
3. **Use consistent dose** — If dose varies, puck isn't uniform
4. **Dial in dose first** — Consistency in weight = consistency in puck

**Test shot:** Even pressure curve, no sudden spikes/drops

---

### Problem 3: UNDEREXTRACTED (sour, thin, weak)

**Looks like:**
- Shot time < 22 seconds
- Pressure rises slowly (lazy ramp)
- Flow rate is HIGH (3-4+ ml/sec early)
- Taste: sour, underdeveloped, thin

**What's happening:**
- Grind is TOO COARSE
- Water passes through too quickly
- Not enough contact time with coffee

**How to fix:**
1. **Grind finer** — Smaller on the grinder
2. **Tamp harder** — Increase tamping pressure
3. **Use finer profile** — Switch to profile with longer plateau
4. **Check water quality** — Very hard water extracts slower (weird but true)

**Test shot:** Aim for 25-28 sec, steady pressure climb to 9 bar

---

### Problem 4: OVEREXTRACTED (bitter, harsh, unbalanced)

**Looks like:**
- Shot time > 32 seconds
- Pressure stays high (no decline)
- Flow rate drops to 1-2 ml/sec
- Output is dark, thick
- Taste: bitter, harsh, unbalanced

**What's happening:**
- Grind is TOO FINE
- Water takes too long to push through
- Extracting bad flavors (caffeine, tannins)

**How to fix:**
1. **Grind coarser** — Go larger on your grinder
2. **Tamp lighter** — Reduce tamping pressure
3. **Use declining profile** — Lower pressure at end to stop extraction
4. **Reduce dose** — Less coffee = faster flow = shorter time

**Test shot:** Aim for 25-28 sec with smooth decline in pressure at the end

---

### Problem 5: TEMPERATURE SWINGS (hot/cold spots)

**Looks like:**
- Temperature line bounces up and down
- Output varies from shot to shot
- Taste: inconsistent, sometimes sour then bitter

**What's happening:**
- Machine isn't stabilized
- Thermal mass issues
- Water hasn't reached stable temp yet

**How to fix:**
1. **Wait longer after powering on** — Minimum 10 minutes, ideally 20
2. **Run flush water** — 6 seconds of hot water through group head before shot
3. **Backflush** — (if your machine supports it) clears cool water from lines
4. **Use "Keep Hot" schedule** — Set machine to warm up before your usual time

**Test shot:** Stable temperature line, within 1-2°C of goal

## The Dial-In Workflow (Step by Step)

### Day 1: Baseline Shot

1. **Grind a dose** — 18g is standard, weigh it
2. **Distribute & tamp** — Flat, level, 25kg pressure
3. **Pull a shot** — Record everything
4. **Taste it** — Note sour? Bitter? Thin? Balanced?
5. **Check the numbers:**
   - Time: 20 sec? (too fast) 30 sec? (too slow)
   - Pressure: Smooth curve or jumpy?
   - Flow: Consistent or erratic?

### Day 2: First Adjustment

**If shot was too fast (< 22 sec):**
- **Grind finer** — Go 1-2 clicks finer
- **Pull another shot**
- **Compare:** Did time increase? By how much?
- **Repeat until** you hit 25-28 sec

**If shot was too slow (> 30 sec):**
- **Grind coarser** — Go 1-2 clicks coarser
- **Pull another shot**
- **Compare:** Did time decrease?
- **Repeat until** you hit 25-28 sec

### Day 3: Refine Flavor

Once you're in the **25-28 second window:**

- **If sour:** Grind a tiny bit finer (0.5 click)
- **If bitter:** Grind a tiny bit coarser (0.5 click)
- **If balanced:** You're done! Save this as a baseline

### Ongoing: Profile Tweaking

Now that grind is dialed, adjust PRESSURE to fine-tune flavor:

- **More sweetness:** Increase pressure (9.5 bar), extend plateau
- **Less acidity:** Add decline at end (ramp down to 7-8 bar)
- **More body:** Lower pressure (8 bar) or slower flow
- **More clarity:** Higher pressure (9.5 bar) or faster flow

## The Master Adjustments

**Need to:**                | **Do this:**
---------------------------|---------------------------
Get time from 20→25 sec    | Grind finer
Get time from 30→25 sec    | Grind coarser
Remove sourness            | Grind finer + add decline
Remove bitterness          | Grind coarser + reduce pressure
Increase sweetness         | Extend plateau at 9 bar
Reduce acidity             | Decline pressure at end
Speed up extraction        | Coarsen grind + lower pressure
Slow down extraction       | Fine grind + higher pressure

## Common Myths (Busted)

### Myth: "Tamp harder = better shot"
**Reality:** Tamp pressure is less important than grind consistency. Standard is 25kg. Too hard (30kg+) can cause issues. Let grind size do the work.

### Myth: "More time = better extraction"
**Reality:** 25-28 seconds is ideal. 35+ seconds = overextraction (bitter). Extraction happens in the first 20 seconds; after that you're pulling bad flavors.

### Myth: "You need an expensive grinder"
**Reality:** A $100 grinder that's consistent beats a $1000 grinder that's inconsistent. Consistency > precision.

### Myth: "Temperature is everything"
**Reality:** Temperature matters, but grind size and pressure matter more. Get machine to thermal stability first; dial grind after.

### Myth: "The same grind works for all beans"
**Reality:** Light roasts need finer grind, dark roasts need coarser. Roast date matters. Beans age and gush less over time.

## The Golden Rules

1. **Change ONE thing at a time** — Grind finer, pull a shot, evaluate. Don't change grind + tamp + pressure all at once.
2. **Record everything** — Your memory is terrible. Write down dose, time, taste, grind setting.
3. **Consistency beats perfection** — A "good enough" shot repeated 10 times is better than a perfect shot once.
4. **Taste > Numbers** — Numbers are guides. Trust your palate.
5. **Give it 3 shots** — First shot with new grind setting is often off. Third shot is usually dialed.

## Next Level: Profile Tuning

Once you're dialed in on a profile, you can experiment:

### Light Roast Profile
- Higher pressure (9.5 bar)
- Longer plateau (20+ sec)
- Minimal decline
- Goal: Extract brightness + sweetness

### Dark Roast Profile
- Lower pressure (8 bar)
- Shorter plateau (15 sec)
- Significant decline (to 6 bar)
- Goal: Avoid bitterness

### Espresso Blend Profile
- Medium pressure (9 bar)
- Medium plateau (18 sec)
- Gentle decline (to 8 bar)
- Goal: Balance

## Troubleshooting Checklist

Before adjusting grind, ask:

- [ ] Is my machine warmed up? (10+ minutes)
- [ ] Did I flush the group head? (6 sec)
- [ ] Is my dose consistent? (within 0.5g)
- [ ] Did I distribute evenly? (no clumps)
- [ ] Is my tamp flat and straight? (not tilted)
- [ ] Am I using fresh beans? (< 4 weeks old)
- [ ] Is my grinder clean? (no old grounds)

If all checked, THEN adjust grind size.
